# users.md — Example (Fictional Company: NestWise, a home services marketplace)

> **This is an example file.** It shows what a completed users.md looks like for a fictional company called NestWise. Use it as a reference, then fill in your own users.md with your actual personas and research.

---

## Primary persona — "The Stressed Homeowner"
**Name / label:** Jordan, the First-Time Homeowner
**Role / title:** Primary household decision-maker for home maintenance (28–45, homeowner for less than 5 years)
**Company type:** N/A — consumer
**Core job-to-be-done:** Find a reliable, fairly-priced professional to fix a home problem quickly without getting ripped off
**Top 3 pain points:**
1. Trust is broken — they've had bad experiences with unreliable contractors and don't know how to vet someone new
2. Pricing is opaque — they don't know what a fair price looks like so they can't tell if they're being overcharged
3. Scheduling is friction-heavy — coordinating availability between their schedule and a pro's takes too many back-and-forth messages
**What success looks like for them:** Describes the problem once, gets 2–3 vetted pros with real reviews and upfront pricing, books a confirmed appointment in under 10 minutes
**Where they live in the product today:** Search, pro profiles, booking flow, review submission

---

## Secondary persona — "The Property Manager"
**Name / label:** Alex, the Small Portfolio Landlord
**Role / title:** Owner of 2–5 rental properties, manages maintenance requests themselves
**Core job-to-be-done:** Keep maintenance costs predictable and response times fast without hiring a full-time property manager
**Key difference from primary:** Less emotional about the decision, highly motivated by speed and cost consistency; more likely to use bulk booking and repeat-pro features

---

## Secondary persona — "The Service Pro"
**Name / label:** Marco, Independent Plumber
**Role / title:** Owner-operator of a small plumbing business (1–3 employees)
**Core job-to-be-done:** Fill his schedule with quality jobs and reduce time spent on estimates that don't convert
**Key difference from primary:** Supply-side user of our pro dashboard; cares about lead quality and booking conversion, not the consumer discovery experience

---

## Anti-persona (who we are NOT building for)
**Description:** Large commercial property managers or construction firms needing project-scale work
**Why we're not targeting them:** Our pro network, pricing model, and booking flow are optimized for residential jobs under $5K; commercial scale requires a fundamentally different product and is explicitly out of scope through v2

---

## Key user insights
- "I just want someone to show up when they say they will" — user interview, Jordan T., February 2025
- 61% of surveyed users said they had abandoned a booking after receiving a quote that felt too vague — NPS survey, Q1 2025
- Scheduling back-and-forth is the #1 CS ticket category — CS ticket analysis, January 2025
- Homeowners who book a second job within 90 days have 41% higher 12-month retention than one-time bookers — data analysis, December 2024
- "I don't know if I'm getting a fair price until after the job is done" — usability study, 6 of 9 participants, March 2025
