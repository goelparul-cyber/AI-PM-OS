# voice.md — Example (Fictional Company: NestWise, a home services marketplace)

> **This is an example file.** It shows what a completed voice.md looks like for a fictional company called NestWise. Use it as a reference, then fill in your own voice.md with your team's actual principles, tone, and vocabulary.

---

## Writing principles
1. Lead with the homeowner, not the feature — every doc opens with the problem a real homeowner has, not what we're building
2. Metrics before adjectives — "reduces booking time by 4 minutes on average" beats "significantly improves the experience"
3. Short sentences in exec and XFN docs — if a sentence needs a comma, consider splitting it
4. Name the tradeoff — every recommendation must acknowledge what we're not doing and why
5. Cite your sources inline — claims in PRDs link to the interview, ticket, or data point that backs them

---

## Tone
**Internal docs (PRDs, specs):** Direct and confident. No hedging ("might," "could potentially"). If something is uncertain, say it's uncertain and name what would resolve it.

**Exec updates:** Metrics-first, outcome-framed. Maya reads these in 90 seconds — put the number and the so-what in sentence one.

**XFN updates:** Collaborative and specific. Lead with what's done and what's needed from them. No blame, no vague asks.

**Partner-facing:** Warmer tone, business-outcome focused — frame everything in terms of bookings, utilization, and ROI.

---

## Vocabulary

### Use these words / phrases
- "homeowners" (not "users" in consumer-facing copy)
- "booking" (not "purchase" — we're a service marketplace, not a store)
- "job" (not "task" — homeowners hire for jobs, not tasks)
- "match rate" (our internal term for % of requests matched to a pro within 24 hours)
- "MAH" (Monthly Active Homeowners — our north star metric)

### Avoid these words / phrases
- "leverage" — use "use" or "apply"
- "learnings" — use "what we learned"
- "delight" — overused; show the specific outcome instead
- "seamless" — means nothing; describe what the flow actually feels like
- "best-in-class" — we don't use this internally ever
- "guaranteed match" — flagged by legal; use "match estimate" or "typically matched within 24 hours"

---

## Format defaults
**PRDs:** H2 headers, prose for problem statement (no bullets), tables for success metrics and scope, bullets only in "What we're NOT doing" and open questions

**Exec updates:** 1 page max. Bold the headline metric. No headers — just 3–4 short paragraphs.

**XFN updates:** Bullet-first. Bold the ask. Never longer than 300 words.

**Length norms:** PRD = 3–5 pages, exec update = 1 page, XFN update = <300 words, interview synthesis = 1–2 pages

## Example of great writing from this team
> "72% of homeowners who requested a plumber abandoned the flow when they couldn't get a same-day confirmation. They were ready to book — they just couldn't get certainty fast enough. This feature brings the confirmation to the moment of intent, not the follow-up email."
